**What's in the box**

- **3D scene** — Three.js with OrbitControls. Sun + 8 planets at their real orbits; the 42,075 near-Earth asteroids rendered as a single InstancedMesh whose positions are computed on the **GPU** by a custom vertex shader (Kepler's equation solved in GLSL via Newton iteration). One uniform `uTime` updates the whole field.
- **Comets** — ~4,000 comets (2,306 hyperbolic) as a separate InstancedMesh; the shader branches into hyperbolic Kepler when `e ≥ 1`.
- **Time** — play/pause, ±10/±30-day buttons, scrub, jump-to-date, "Now", speed from 0.5 days/s to 10 years/s. Keyboard: Space, ←/→, F to follow, Esc to deselect.
- **Filters** — PHA-only, Sentry-only, by class (AMO/APO/ATE/IEO), min diameter, max MOID, search by name/designation/SPK-ID, comets toggle, planet labels, orbit lines.
- **Detail panel** — physical + orbital properties, closest close approaches (sorted), and the CNEOS Sentry impact-risk summary for monitored objects.
- **Focus & follow** — camera retargets on a selected body each frame.
- **Deep links** — every state change updates the URL hash. `#t=2460600.5&s=433&sp=5&fp=1` jumps to a date, opens the Eros detail panel, sets 5× speed, and applies PHA-only — all on load.
- **Visual encoding** — color by class, PHA in red, Sentry in orange. Scale bar always shows the current zoom level in au (or km when zoomed in).

**Data flow**

Raw JSON (~28 MB) → `scripts/preprocess-data.mjs` at build time → compact Float32 binaries + string tables + a sorted close-approach index (~6 MB extra) → fetched at runtime. The 16 MB `asteroids.json` becomes ~6 MB of binary that goes straight into GPU buffers.
