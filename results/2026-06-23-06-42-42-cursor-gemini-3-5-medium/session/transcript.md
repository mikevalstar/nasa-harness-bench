### What I Built

#### 1. The Core 3D Celestial Engine (`src/components/SolarSystemCanvas.tsx`)
- **Interactive 3D Viewport**: Uses Three.js to render the Sun, the 8 planets, their orbits, and Near-Earth Objects.
- **High-Performance Rendering**: Renders all **~42,000 asteroids** at solid 60+ FPS using a single `THREE.Points` particle system and flat typed arrays (`Float32Array`).
- **Mathematical Accuracy**: Rather than drawing hardcoded or static paths, each body is propagated in real-time inside the rendering frame based on its exact orbital elements in the **J2000 ecliptic frame** from its unique `epoch` (using Julian Dates).
- **Physics Propagation Solver (`src/utils/propagation.ts`)**:
  - **Elliptical Orbits ($e < 0.999$)**: Solves Kepler's equation ($E - e \sin E = M$) using a highly optimized Newton-Raphson loop.
  - **Parabolic Orbits ($0.999 \le e \le 1.001$)**: Solves Barker's equation ($x^3 + 3x - 2A = 0$) analytically, ensuring stability for highly eccentric or near-open orbits.
  - **Hyperbolic Orbits ($e > 1.001$)**: Solves the hyperbolic Kepler's equation ($e \sinh F - F = N$) via Newton-Raphson, letting you visualize comets on open orbits wrapping around the Sun and flying back out into space.
- **Smart Scaling**: Orbit positions are 100% physically accurate (1 unit = 1 AU). Planet sphere dimensions are scaled up using a customizable visual slider (1,000x to 15,000x) so they are visible alongside their orbit lines.
- **Click Selection**: Integrated a `THREE.Raycaster` with custom point thresholds to let users select any asteroid, planet, or comet by clicking on its point or sphere on the screen.
- **Camera Focus & Follow**: Toggling "Lock Camera" dynamically updates the `OrbitControls` focus point to track the selected body in real-time as the simulation advances.

#### 2. Fully Interactive Time & HUD Controls (`src/App.tsx`)
- **Interactive Playback**: Play/Pause, speed adjustment (0.1, 1, 10, 30, and 365 simulation days per real second).
- **Timeline Scrubber**: A timeline slider letting users scrub time backwards/forwards seamlessly between the years 2010 and 2040.
- **Calendar Picker**: Integrated a native date-input picker showing Universal Time (UT) to let users jump to any calendar date instantly.
- **Dynamic HTML Projection Labels**: Absolute-positioned HTML overlays for planet labels and selected target vectors. By projecting 3D positions onto the 2D screen and directly mutating DOM style transforms on-frame (ref-based), the application bypasses React's virtual DOM re-render overhead completely.

#### 3. Search, Filter, and Intelligence Sidebar
- **Real-Time Search**: Search across ~42,000 objects instantly by name or JPL designation.
- **Orbit Classification Filters**: Filter objects by JPL orbit class:
  - **Apollo (APO)**: Earth-crossing orbits with $a > 1.0$ AU.
  - **Aten (ATE)**: Earth-crossing orbits with $a < 1.0$ AU.
  - **Amor (AMO)**: Earth-approaching orbits (outer-tangential).
  - **Atira (IEO)**: Orbits fully inside Earth's orbit.
- **Hazard & Metric Constraints**:
  - Filter for Potentially Hazardous Asteroids (PHAs) only.
  - Filter by minimum size/estimated diameter (All, >100m, >1km).
  - Filter by Maximum Minimum Orbit Intersection Distance (MOID) with Earth in AU.

#### 4. Advanced Scientific Details Panel & Overlays
- **2D Top-Down Orbit Schematic (`src/components/OrbitSchematic.tsx`)**: When an object is selected, a custom 2D Canvas renders a local projection of its orbit path compared to Earth's orbit, indicating their exact position vectors and drawing a direct distance vector between the object and Earth.
- **Physical Properties Card**: Displays absolute magnitude ($H$), estimated diameter, albedo, rotation period, spectral type, and first observation date.
- **Earth Close-Approach Registry**: Loads `close-approaches.json` and parses approaches for the selected object. The list automatically sorts to highlight the closest encounter near the active simulation date, indicating the approach distance in both AU and **Lunar Distances (LD)**.
- **Sentry Impact Risk Overlay**: Merges CNEOS impact monitoring data from `sentry.json` onto the selected object, showing an alert box detailing cumulative impact probability, Torino/Palermo scales, risk window, and potential impact count.
- **Comets Overlay**: Toggles an overlay displaying ~4,000 comets from `comets.json` with purple orbit visual cues, utilizing the robust hyperbolic/parabolic solvers to propagate their complex open trajectories.
- **Deep Linking**: Syncs active simulation state (Julian Date, selection, comets toggle, camera mode, class filters, size scales) directly to the URL hash, making it easy to share or bookmark a specific moment.
